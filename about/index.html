About

Dr. Goldberg completed her undergraduate education at UC San Diego, earning a B.S. in Biochemistry and Cell Biology, and a B.A. in Political Science. She furthered her education at UCLA where she earned a Masters Degree in Public Health/Epidemiology. Dr. Goldberg then completed her medical degree at Finch University of Health Sciences/The Chicago Medical School and returned to Los Angeles to complete her internship and residency training in Pediatrics at the Mattel Children's Hospital at UCLA.

Throughout her academic career, Dr. Goldberg has maintained a public health focus and has presented her research on childhood obesity and HIV/AIDS at national conferences.

In 2005, she designed childhood safety guidelines for the largest national specialty retailer of juvenile products for infants and young children. More recently, Dr. Goldberg participates in office-based research studies in coordination with the American Academy of Pediatrics where the ultimate goal is to improve the health of all children.

Dr. Goldberg currently practices as a general pediatrician in a large and extremely busy pediatric practice in Encino, California caring for newborns, college students, and all ages in between. She has been on active staff at a variety of hospitals/medical centers throughout the Los Angeles area which has given her the opportunity to not only teach medical residents but give seminars to soon-to-be parents.

When not spending time with her family, which includes three children ages 2-8 years, she also enjoys blogging about any topic related to children and their overall well-being.
